About Me

My technical design skills were applied at academic institutions from Lehigh University, NYU and Pratt Institute. My experience at those schools introduced me to new technologies such as AR (Vuforia, Google AR Core, Apple ARKit 2) and VR (Unity 3D, Oculus SDK, and Android SDK), focusing on the development process with open frameworks and prototyping procedures. At Pratt Institute I am in charge of creating development workflows for various VR and AR headsets and maintaining Pratt’s wordpress CMS. Faculty and staff require apps and special software to use for school promotions or faculty projects. I know how to develop software for Windows Mixed Reality headsets, Google Daydream headsets, Oculus Go and Rift headsets, HTC Vive headsets, Magic Leap One, Meta 2 and iOS and Android phone development. My passion is developing products for people to use. I love designing and producing content. Whether the industry is in education or a corporation, I am highly adaptable in the process of creating something from nothing. I work well with teams and have been managing a student team at Pratt for the development process for VR and AR apps.

Projects

Pratt Research Open House

The Research Open House App is an app created so that visitors of the Research Open House can tour Pratt’s research event without a tour guide. The way it is designed, it will be more individualized, with different types of tours suited for different people’s interests and needs. It is meant to make Pratt more accessible to the people who are touring it, so that the campus really feels like an “open house.”

Polygonia

This is a puzzle game centered around treating children with autism. The background for the theme of the game comes from the mental health field. Virtual Reality Therapy or commonly known as VRT, is starting to become a new type therapy for some mental illnesses treatment. Polygonia is a game that will be catered to the mental illness demographic but eventually can lead into something that the general population can use.
